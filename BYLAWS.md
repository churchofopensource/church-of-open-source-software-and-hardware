# Bylaws

## Article I - Name and Location

### 1.01 Name

The name of this corporation or legal entity (COLE) shall be Church of Open Source Software and Hardware (Denomination #1772732140).

The business of this COLE may be conducted as Church of Open Source Software and Hardware (Denomination #1772732140) or as Church of Open Source #1772732140.

### 1.02 Aliases

For the purposes of these Bylaws (sometimes BYLAWS), Church of Open Source Software and Hardware (Denomination #1772732140) or Church of Open Source #1772732140 may also be referenced as the Church of Open Source Software and Hardware, or as this Denomination of the Church of Open Source Software and Hardware, or as this Denomination of the Church, or as the Church, or as Church.

### 1.03 Location

The principle registered agent has current residency in the State of Arizona, United States.  The Church of Open Source Software and Hardware has no physical office building.  The Church website is where these Bylaws, the Core Beliefs, and related documents and public records are published.

### 1.04 Principle Registered Agent

The principle registered agent is Thomas Hruska, a resident of the State of Arizona, United States.  The principle registered agent is identified and designated as Supreme Cleric of the Church of Open Source Software and Hardware (Denomination #1772732140) or as the Supreme Cleric or as Supreme Cleric.

## Article II - Purposes and Legal Powers

### 2.01 Purpose

This Denomination of the Church of Open Source Software and Hardware is a Sole Proprietorship that shall be operated exclusively for educational, research, and civil rights purposes according to its Core Beliefs within the allowed meaning of the laws of the state in which the Church operates.

The Church of Open Source Software and Hardware is a religious organization that respects and promotes software and hardware freedoms and related civil rights and liberties.

### 2.02 Powers

The Supreme Cleric shall have the power, directly or indirectly, alone or in conjunction or in cooperation with others, to do any and all lawful acts which may be necessary or convenient to affect the educational, research, and/or civil rights purposes, for which the COLE is organized, and to aid or assist other organizations or persons whose activites further accomplish, foster, or attain such purposes.

The Legal powers of the Supreme Cleric and COLE prohibit acceptance of any direct financial contributions for this Denomination of the Church including, but not limited to, money, cryptocurrency, and declared in-kind physical goods and services.

### 2.03 Dissolution

The Supreme Cleric may opt to retire and dissolve this Denomination of the Church if or when the Supreme Cleric decides the responsiblities of running this Denomination of the Church becomes too great.

This Denomination of the Church is also automatically retired and dissolved upon the permanent physical death of the Supreme Cleric.

## Article III - Membership

### 3.01 No Membership Classes

The COLE shall have no membership class and no members who have any right to vote or title or interest to the COLE nor to the Supreme Cleric.

### 3.02 Non-Voting Special Affiliates

The Supreme Cleric may approve classes of non-voting special affiliates with rights, privileges, and obligations established by the Supreme Cleric.  Special affiliates may be individuals, businesses, and other organizations that seek to support the mission and purpose of the COLE.  The Supreme Cleric shall have the sole authority to both admit and remove any individual, business, or other organization as a special affiliate, to recognize representatives of special affiliates, and to make determinations as to special affiliates' rights, privileges, and obligations.

Other than the name of the special affiliate, at no time shall special affiliate personally identifiable information (PII) be shared nor distributed nor sold to other individuals, businesses, other organzations, or groups.  At the sole discretion of the Supreme Cleric, special affiliates may be given endorsement, recognition, and media coverage at various events or on the Internet.  Special affiliates have no voting rights and are not members of the COLE.

### 3.03 Non-Voting Regular Affiliates

Regular affiliates may be individuals, businesses, and other organizations that seek to support the mission and purpose of the COLE.  Anyone may choose for themselves to become a regular affiliate at any time and adopt the regular affiliate class that best represents their affiliation.  Anyone may choose for themselves to cease and terminate being a regular affiliate at any time.  The allowed methods of adoption and termination of regular affiliation shall be defined in a published document as a public record on the Church website.

The Supreme Cleric may approve additional classes of non-voting regular affiliates with rights, privileges, and obligations established by the Supreme Cleric.

Regular affiliates have no voting rights and are not members of non-voting special affiliates and are not members of the COLE.

### 3.03.01 Congregants

The non-voting regular affiliate congregant class shall exist and be collectively identified and designated as Congregants.

### 3.03.02 Preachers

The non-voting regular affiliate preacher class shall exist and be collectively identified and designated as Preachers.  A preacher has released at least one open source software or hardware product such as, but not limited to, source code, schematics, diagrams, and/or documentation in line with the mission and purpose of the COLE.

### 3.03.03 Elders

The non-voting regular affiliate elder class shall exist and be collectively identified and designated as Elders.  An elder has released at least one tutorial or historical fascination to teach the next generation of Preachers about one esoteric topic of computing such as, but not limited to, obscure programming language or operating system quirks, lost histories of computing, and/or personal historical computing stories no one is likely interested in.

A Council of Elders consists of two or more Elders that may periodically convene to bemoan the state of open source software and/or hardware and/or the state of computing at various locations including, but not limited to, conferences, hacker groups, meetings, meetups, issue trackers, podcasts, comments sections of published videos and articles, and/or social media.

### 3.03.04 Televangelists

The non-voting regular affiliate televangelist class shall exist and be collectively identified and designated as Televangelists.  A televangelist promotes open source software and/or hardware without having ever directly run a compiler from the terminal and/or directly soldered two components together.  A televangelist might frequently and assertively mispronounce words and technical jargon.

## Article IV - Supreme Cleric

### 4.01 Administrative Steward

The Supreme Cleric is the administrative steward of the Bylaws, Core Beliefs, and Church website.  Changes to the Bylaws and Core Beliefs are considered to be major actions of the administrative steward to be approved, signed (a digital signature such as using a private key during the publishing step is permitted), and dated by the Supreme Cleric and committed to the official records of the COLE.  The administrative steward is responsible for maintaining the correctness and accuracy of those things under their stewardship to the best of their ability and to further the mission and purpose of the COLE.  These responsibilities may not be delegated to anyone else except through Power of Attorney, which must be disclosed as a major action (see 6.01).

The Church website is an important function of the administrative steward which therefore may require the administrative steward to pay relevant, nominal domain name and hosting fees associated with running a website from the personal funds of the administrative steward that are most likely not tax-deductible and also not recoverable due to the fundamental design of the structure, mission and purpose, and legal powers of the Church.  There are many no cost and low-cost options for website hosting available and the administrative steward is free to choose the hosting environment and platform for the Church website for this Denomination of the Church.

The administrative steward may be required to pay relevant, nominal, periodic fees from the personal funds of the administrative steward associated with running a legal business entity that are most likely not tax-deductible and also not recoverable due to the fundamental design of the structure, mission and purpose, and legal powers of the Church (e.g. see 4.04).

The administrative steward may, in certain circumstances, opt to recover any personal financial expenses occurred according to 5.02 Mandatory Indemnification.

### 4.02 Financial Compensation

The Supreme Cleric is prohibited from accepting financial compensation within the direct role of Supreme Cleric.  Financial compensation includes, but is not limited to, money, cryptocurrency, and declared in-kind physical goods and services.

The Supreme Cleric may work for an employer or be self-employed separately from the direct role of Supreme Cleric.  Special care is to always be taken to separate the Supreme Cleric role and Church from other personal financial interests.

### 4.03 Autonomy

The Supreme Cleric is autonomous and not beholden to any special interests.  The Supreme Cleric is both free to act and free to not act on behalf of all Article III classes as the Supreme Cleric sees fit within the mission and purpose of the COLE.

### 4.04 Missionary

The Supreme Cleric is the missionary who plants their Demonination of the Church in their physical geographic area/region.  The Supreme Cleric is responsible for ensuring the completion of valid registration, licensing, and maintenance thereof for their Denomination of the Church with the relevant local/state legal entities as required and as necessary by local/state law.  This is actually a critical function of the administrative steward which therefore may require the administrative steward to pay relevant, nominal, periodic business registration and registration renewal fees from the personal funds of the administrative steward that are most likely not tax-deductible and also not recoverable due to the fundamental design of the structure, mission and purpose, and legal powers of the Church.

### 4.05 Attestation

The Supreme Cleric must attest to an appropriate dated affirmation of the lawful and religious duties as Supreme Cleric along with its locality/state of legal registration of the Denomination.  The attestation must be published and made available as a public record on the Church website of the COLE.

### 4.06 Term of Office

The Supreme Cleric of this Denomination of the Church is a lifetime position until an officially declared dissolution of this Denomination of the Church by the Supreme Cleric or the permanent physical death of the Supreme Cleric, whichever comes first.  Further, the position of Supreme Cleric of this Denomination of the Church is not transferrable nor can be delegated to another party except through Power of Attorney, which must be disclosed as a major action.  See 2.03 and 6.01.

## Article V - Contracts and Indemnification

### 5.01 - Contracts and Other Writings

All contracts and other agreements of the COLE shall be executed by the administrative steward and signed by the Supreme Cleric.

Contracts and other agreements must state they have no financial value to ensure compliance with the legal powers of the Supreme Cleric and the COLE (see 2.02) and financial compensation of the Supreme Cleric (see 4.02).

### 5.02 - Mandatory Indemnification

The administrative steward shall be indemnified who was wholly successful, on the merits or otherwise, in the defense of any proceeding against this Denomination of the Church to which the administrative steward was a party against reasonable expenses incurred by the admininstrative steward in connection with the proceeding.

Indemnifcation of the previous shall be executed thusly:  The administrative steward must reveal the exact personal expenses incurred used in the defense in connection with the relevant proceeding(s) and then may run a personal campaign to fully replenish personal funds for the exact value after taxes of those personal expenses.  The administrative steward will subsequently publish details of the relevant proceeding(s) and associated expense information on the Church website as a major action to be approved, signed (a digital signature such as using a private key during the publishing step is permitted), and dated by the Supreme Cleric and committed to the official public records of the COLE.

The administrative steward shall be indemnified in any proceeding involving the production of witness testimony, especially as an expert witness, by the Supreme Cleric on behalf of all Article III classes for this Denomination of the Church against reasonable expenses incurred by the admininstrative steward in connection with the proceeding.

Indemnifcation of the previous shall be executed thusly:  The administrative steward must reveal the exact personal expenses incurred involving the production of witness testimony by the Supreme Cleric on behalf of all Article III classes in connection with the relevant proceeding(s) and then may fully replenish personal funds for the exact value after taxes of those personal expenses by running a personal campaign or other legal means of recovery.  The administrative steward will subsequently publish details of the relevant proceeding(s) and associated expense information on the Church website as a major action to be approved, signed (a digital signature such as using a private key during the publishing step is permitted), and dated by the Supreme Cleric and committed to the official public records of the COLE.

## Article VI - Miscellaneous

### 6.01 - Books and Records

The COLE shall keep correct books and records of account and shall keep a public record of all major actions taken by the Supreme Cleric, a public record of all major actions taken by the administrative steward, and a public record of all recovered personal financial expenses by the administrative steward.  In addition, the COLE shall keep an up to date copy of the public records, and Bylaws and Core Beliefs as amended by to date on the Church website.

### 6.02 - Fiscal Year

The fiscal year of the COLE shall be January 1 through December 31 of each year.

In general, the fiscal year of the COLE is not particularly relevant except for Article V.

## Article VII - Document Retention Policy

### 7.01 - Purpose

To establish standards for document integrity, retention, and destruction, and to promote the proper treatment of Church records for the COLE.

### 7.02 - Exception For Litigation

If the administrative steward is notified of pending litigation or the Supreme Cleric determines a specific record is relevant to litigation or pending litigation or potential litigation, then that record is to be preserved until it is determined that the record is no longer needed.  This exception supercedes any established destruction schedule for those records.

### 7.03 - Default Retention Period

The default minimum retention period is that which is required by law.  The default maximum retention period is that which is required by law plus one year.

The public records (see 6.01), Bylaws, and Core Beliefs are to be retained permanently unless and until this Denomination of the Church is dissolved (see 2.03).

For all other documents/records, when in doubt, assume a minimum of 7 years for physical and digital documents/records and 10 years for legal documents/records.  Physical documents/records may be digitized and stored in a reasonable digital format (e.g. PDF) as long as the content in the digital format is clearly legible.  The Supreme Cleric has autonomy (see 4.03) to decide the retention period for any specific category of document/record not expressly specified.

### 7.04 - Email

Email may be deleted once that email is no longer deemed relevant to a particular conversation.  The Supreme Cleric has autonomy (see 4.03) to decide the retention period for any given email conversation.
