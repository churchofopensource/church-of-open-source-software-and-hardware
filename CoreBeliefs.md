# Core Beliefs

## Article I - Fundamental Human Right to Actually Own Our Own Property

### 1.01 - Statement

We believe in the fundamental human right to own the devices we purchase and software we obtain a valid license for and to use them both for any lawful purpose we so choose to use them for.

We further believe in the fundamental human right to be able to purchase hardware and license software outright to use in perpetuity.

### 1.02 - Rationale

Governments and corporations are consistently interested in obtaining and maintaining remote control over the very hardware devices we purchased with our money and software we legally acquired a license for.

That necessitates establishing a bright line boundary:  Once purchased, the device is ours to do with as we please in perpetuity and is not yours to control.  Once licensed, the software is also ours to do with as we please in perpetuity and is not yours to control.  Subscription based software and hardware may exist but only if there are also reasonable perpetual options available by the same organization.

### 1.03 - Exception

Should an individual use their devices and/or software for unlawful purposes and is proven to have done so in a court of law through trial by jury, then that particular individual may be subsequently subject to any laws that require their devices or software to be monitored and/or controlled.  Broad surveillance of a population for potential crimes that someone might commit, however, is not part of this exception.  An individual has to be found guilty of a crime in a court of law through trial by jury for this exception to apply.

## Article II - Fundamental Human Right to Personal Privacy

### 2.01 - Statement

We believe in the fundamental human right to personal privacy when it involves personal property and personal data.

### 2.02 - Rationale

Governments and corporations are consistently interested in obtaining control over and monitoring every aspect of a person's life.  Computer technology, both software and hardware, and, in particular, the Internet has accelerated towards a true police state faster than anything else in human history.

That necessitates establishing a bright line boundary:  What devices we own, what data we store on our own devices, and what software we choose to run on our own devices is our own personal business and no one else's.

### 2.03 - Exception

Should an individual use their devices and/or software for unlawful purposes, then that particular individual may be subject to any laws that require them to disclose their private information and data during the discovery phase of their jury trial in a court of law.  Broad surveillance of a population for potential crimes that someone might commit, however, is not part of this exception.  An individual has to be subjected to a trial by jury in a court of law for this exception to apply.

## Article III - Fundamental Human Right to Remain Anonymous by Default

### 3.01 - Statement

We believe in the fundamental human right to individual anonymity by default.

### 3.02 - Rationale

Governments and corporations are consistently interested in obtaining the identity of individuals for nefarious purposes including, but not limited to, limiting access to technology including hardware and software, limiting access to information, tracking behaviors, suppressing dissent, political campaigning, and advertising products and services.

That necessitates establishing a bright line boundary:  Anonymity is the default expectation.  When it comes to governments and corporations, we are not your best buds, not your chums, nor your friends, but also not your enemies nor adversaries and the correct default relationship with us is to let us be invisible, anonymous, and at an arm's length.  Most people just want to live out normal lives.

### 3.03 - Exception

Should an individual use anonymity for unlawful purposes, then that particular individual may be subject to any laws that require them to remove their right to anonymity during the discovery phase of their jury trial in a court of law and, upon conviction, subject to any laws that require them to not be anonymous henceforth.  Broad surveillance of a population for potential crimes that someone might commit, however, is not part of this exception.  An individual has to be found guilty of a crime in a court of law through trial by jury for this exception to apply.

## Article IV - Fundamental Human Right to Repair and Modify and Create Our Own Devices and Hardware and Software

### 4.01 - Statement

We believe in the fundamental human right that every device we purchase is ours and every hardware component in every device we purchase should be easily repaired/replaced using commonly available tools with affordable repair parts and freely available schematics.

We further believe in the fundamental human right that every device we purchase is ours to modify in any way we desire, especially in software, for personal use and lawful personal benefit without being intentionally hindered from doing so.

### 4.02 - Rationale

Governments and corporations are consistently interested in obtaining control over every aspect of our lives and eat out our substance.  In this instance, the intentional inability to repair our own devices is always by design to control us, block us from creating new things and/or modifying what is rightfully ours (e.g. via DRM), produce more landfill e-waste, and make more profits at the expense of ruining the whole planet.

That necessitates establishing a bright line boundary:  All components of all devices must be easily repaired and replaced with commonly available tools with affordable repair parts and freely available schematics.  A person may choose to take their device back to the original manufacturer for repair and incur the costs and benefits associated with doing so, but that is just one of many options available to the person.  Further, Digital Rights Management (DRM) techonolgies are not just a hindrance but violate our inalienable right to create new property, repair our own property, and even modify our own property for personal use and lawful personal benefit.

### 4.03 - Exceptions

### 4.03.01 - National Defense

A nation has the right to defend itself from adversaries that would seek to destroy its people.  The hardware and software in direct defense related devices may contain Digital Rights Management (DRM) technologies as long as the government has full access to those DRM technologies to be able to make its own repairs to the devices and software it purchases expressly and exclusively for defense related devices and software.  Those DRM technologies may not be weaponized against the population that its government is governing.

### 4.03.02 - Individual Actions

Owning a device or software that is typically used for unlawful purposes is not unlawful in and of itself.  Modifying a device or software in a manner where such modification is typically used for unlawful purposes is not unlawful in and of itself.  Selling devices or software, modified or otherwise, that are typically used for unlawful purposes is not unlawful in and of itself.

Should an individual use a device or software, modified or otherwise, for expressly unlawful purposes, then that particular individual may be subject to relevant laws prohibiting future ownership of such devices or software upon being found guilty in a criminal trial before a jury in a court of law.  Broad surveillance of a population for potential crimes that someone might commit, however, is not part of this exception.  An individual has to be found guilty of a crime in a court of law through trial by jury for this exception to apply.

## Article V - Fundamental Human Right of Access to Affordable Personal Technology

### 5.01 - Statement

We believe in the fundamental human right that access to devices, hardware, and software must be accessible and affordable for personal use for everyone.

### 5.02 - Rationale

Governments and corporations are consistently interested in controlling a population in an Orwellian dystopian surveillance police state as well as ensuring the poor and the disabled are robbed and what little they have given to the wealthy, ultra-wealthy, and able-bodied.  Meanwhile, our ever increasingly interconnected world is becoming more and more daily dependent upon having basic access to technology and the Internet.

That necessitates establishing a bright line boundary:  All smaller manufactured devices, hardware, and software must be affordable for all individuals for personal use.  For larger-scale industrial devices, when it is possible to safely do so, a manufacturer must also have at least one sufficiently capable and reasonably safe consumer variant of the product that is affordable for all individuals for personal use.  A gross profit margin exceeding 50% is not affordable.  Greed benefits no one and harms everyone.  Additionally, care must be taken to ensure devices, hardware, and software are accessible to those of us who are disabled.

### 5.03 - Exception

Should anyone create a device, hardware, and/or software that will knowingly cause great physical harm to another individual or damage their property, then they may be subject to any laws that require them to be punished for crimes they may have committed upon being found guilty through trial by jury in a court of law and may be subsequently subject to laws that monitor their actions henceforth.  Broad surveillance of a population for the potential creation and use of such devices, hardware, and/or software that someone might create and use, however, is not part of this exception.  Each individual involved has to be found guilty of a crime in a court of law through trial by jury for this exception to apply.

## Article VI - Fundamental Human Right to Install and Use and Develop and Test Any Lawful Software From Anywhere of Our Choosing Without Hindrance

### 6.01 - Statement

We believe in the fundamental human right to install and use lawful software from anywhere of our own choice without third-party hindrance.

We futher believe in the fundamental human right to develop and test any lawful software written in any programming language of our own choice without third-party hindrance.

We futher believe in the fundamental human right to upload and/or transfer any such lawful software to anywhere of our own choice for global deployment such that users can install and use said lawful software all without third-party hindrance.

### 6.02 - Rationale

Governments and corporations are consistently interested in controlling a population, robbing that population, and especially robbing software developers through vendor lock-in, blocking the free development and installation of software, and disproportionate taxation and/or profiteering.  The freedom to install software downloaded from websites is continually being chipped away under the false pretense of malware prevention when it is actually about control and greed.

For example, forcing software developers to pay money to develop and publish their software plus a highway robbery app store fee of 30% for in-app purchases plus having to fill out a half-dozen additional surveys every time developers wish to publish an update for their apps plus being forced to use specific programming languages to develop said software apps, instead of being able to freely develop in any programming language in any development environment and publish an app and/or library on a self-hosted website and just download and install/use from there like mother nature intended.

That necessitates establishing a bright line boundary:  All personal devices must support the free development of lawful software applications and libraries in whatever programming language the software developer wishes to develop them in and in whatever development environment they wish to develop such applications and libraries in.  In addition, software developers must have the ability to publish their lawful software applications on their own self-hosted websites and to allow others to download and use those applications and libraries on their own personal devices without any third-party hindrance.

### 6.03 - Exception

Should anyone create or use a device, hardware, and/or software for expressly harmful purposes including, but not limited to, credential theft, financial theft, data theft, data corruption/deletion/encryption, attacking other systems, ransom, and/or keylogging, then they may be subject to any laws that require them to be punished for crimes they may have committed upon being found guilty through trial by jury in a court of law and may be subsequently subject to laws that monitor their actions henceforth.  Broad surveillance of a population for the potential creation or use of such devices, hardware, and/or software that someone might create or use, however, is not part of this exception.  Each individual involved has to be found guilty of a crime in a court of law through trial by jury for this exception to apply.

## Article VII - Fundamental Human Right to Long Average Device Lifetimes

### 7.01 - Statement

We believe in the fundamental human right for devices to have long average lifetimes.

### 7.02 - Rationale

Corporations are consistently interested in controlling a population, robbing that population, harming the planet, reducing choice, and producing lower quality devices, hardware, firmware, and software in the name of profits.  Further, the cost of entry is prohibitive in many industries where a handful of players result in little to no incentive to improve their products and, in fact, regularly collude to reduce the quality of their products to intentionally produce more landfill waste.

For example, major appliances in homes used to last 30+ years and now last only 5-10 years which is by design.  Another example is smartphone manufacturers no longer have a replaceable battery option, thus causing most owners to replace their phone every 3-4 years when the battery stops charging normally.  However, the average phone offers functionally sufficient features and performance for at least 10 years - again, this is by design.  Another example is purchasing brand new RVs, which are frequently broken at the time of purchase and might be repaired 6 months from the time of purchase...at the earliest.

That necessitates establishing a bright line boundary:  All manufacturers of devices and associated hardware, firmware, and/or software must make devices that have long average lifetimes of at least a 20 years guaranteed device lifetime with minor and affordable regular maintenance for all major appliances, vehicles, and other high value purchases and at least a 10 years guaranteed device lifetime with minor and affordable very occasional simple DIY maintenance for all other devices.  Take honest pride in your craft and thus stop producing low quality products that intentionall fall apart or fail in a couple of years.

### 7.03 - Exception

A manufacturer may opt to make a cheaper device and associated hardware, firmware, and/or software as long as they are also already making and continue to produce a similarly functional device with a long average lifetime.  Relevant laws establishing criminal liability must be passed to hold the owners and managers at manufacturers criminally liable for filling landfills with waste while chasing after profits when they fail to produce an equivalent device with a long average lifetime.

## Article VIII - Fundamental Human Right to Performant Optimized Unbloated Software and Energy Efficient Hardware

### 8.01 - Statement

We believe in the fundamental human right for software to be free of bloat and to be optimized to perform well on all kinds of devices and hardware.

We further believe in the fundamental human right for all devices and hardware to be energy efficient.

### 8.02 - Rationale

Corporations and software developers have become irresponsible and extremely lazy and hardware manufacturers have also obliged the laziness by releasing hardware that draws absurd amounts of power from the electric grid.  The end result is massive binary blobs for most software, which are consuming gigawatts of power at scale, effectively using up all of the fresh water on the whole planet to cool massive data centers and thus turning the planet into an unlivable hellscape.

For example, some developers have started bundling a whole web browser with their applications, which result in 100MB+ downloads and consume gigabytes of RAM.  Advertising networks on the web are another egregious example of a bloatware extravaganza, which require making dozens of DNS requests and web requests to deliver just one ad, which results in making hundreds of requests to hundreds of servers per page load for just one webpage with that waste in resources being multiplied across billions of requests across millions of different websites every single day.

That necessitates establishing a bright line boundary:  All software applications, which includes all dependencies, must aim for not exceeding a final 1MB compressed size - smaller is better.  All software must aim to start up from a cold start and be ready to use in under 1000 milliseconds - faster is better.  All software must aim to be as performant as possible and each new version should notably improve performance on similar hardware - faster is better.  All hardware must aim for less than 5W continuous power draw per device at idle - less wattage draw is better.  Additionally, bundling a web browser with an application is not allowed.  Websites and web applications must aim for the bare minimum number of requests to deliver their content and, specifically, no more than one DNS request and one web request for all ad content for a given webpage - fewer DNS requests and web server requests is better.

### 8.03 - Exception

An individual may develop and use bloated, inefficient, unoptimized software for their own personal use on their own devices but may not share their bloated, inefficient, unoptimized software with anyone else nor deploy it to a remote system like a data center.  Do not inflict bloated, inefficient, unoptimized software on the rest of the world.

## Article IX - Fundamental Human Right to Affordable Fiber Optic Internet Access

### 9.01 - Statement

We believe in the fundamental human right to affordable, functional, no data caps, unrestricted, symmetrical, high-speed fiber optic Internet access to every residence.

### 9.02 - Rationale

Corporations, especially telecommunications corporations, are consistently interested in controlling a population, robbing that population, charging outlandish and outrageous fees, providing terrible customer service, blocking high-speed fiber optic deployments using both legal and illegal means, restricting access, restricting and/or limiting information/data, lying about network speeds to both customers and the government, intentionally hiding/obfuscating terrible upstream speeds, collusion with other telecom corporations, and only serving areas in ways that allow them to maximize profits.  These truths were exposed during a certain worldwide shutdown not too long ago where everyone was required to work from home for a couple of years.  Also, some telecoms have allegedly stolen at least $200 billion USD of taxpayer money where they allegedly promised to deploy "fiber to the home" but they also allegedly squandered all of it and were even deregulated instead of doing what they promised to do with the money.  At any rate, owning modern devices, hardware, and software is mostly pointless without also having sufficient Internet access that those devices, hardware, and software can utilize.  Furthermore, fiber optic Internet access to every residence opens up the possibility of reducing or even eliminating the perceived need for data centers to exist.

That necessitates establishing a bright line boundary:  Every residence must have affordable, functional, no data caps, unrestricted, symmetrical, high-speed fiber optic Internet access.

### 9.03 - Exceptions

### 9.03.01 - Reasonable Punishment For Telecoms

To properly incentivize them to deploy fiber optic Internet access everywhere, every telecommunications C-suite executive, VP, and manager should be required to use dial-up Internet until everyone else in the nation has fiber optic Internet access.

For those telecom executives that appear to have allegedly committed mass fraud, a jury should determine guilt and if found guilty of fraud they should be required to pay back every penny the telecoms wrongfully took plus damages and interest with accounting for inflation and maybe even be required to visit the inside of a prison cell for a while too.

### 9.03.02 - Individual Actions

Should an individual use their high-speed Internet connection for expressly unlawful purposes, then that particular individual may be subject to relevant laws limiting their ability to use a high-speed symmetric Internet connection in an unrestricted and/or unsupervised manner upon being found guilty in a criminal trial before a jury in a court of law.  Broad surveillance of a population for potential crimes that someone might commit, however, is not part of this exception.  An individual has to be found guilty of a crime in a court of law through trial by jury for this exception to apply.
