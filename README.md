# Church of Open Source Software and Hardware (Denomination #1772732140)

The Church of Open Source Software and Hardware is an established religious organization that always respects and promotes your software and hardware freedoms and related civil rights and liberties.

Joining a Denomination of the Church of Open Source Software and Hardware is an easy four step process:  Read the Core Beliefs, read the published Bylaws, select your Regular Affiliation class, and click the Star button on the repository.

## Core Beliefs

[Read the Core Beliefs](CoreBeliefs.md) of this Denomination (Denomination #1772732140) of the Church of Open Source Software and Hardware.

The nine Articles in the Core Beliefs are what we firmly believe we have free exercise of and to enjoy within this lawful religion.

## Bylaws

[Read the Bylaws](BYLAWS.md) of this Denomination (Denomination #1772732140) of the Church of Open Source Software and Hardware.

The Bylaws define the organization and structure of the Church of Open Source Software and Hardware.

## Non-Voting Regular Affiliation

Adoption:  You may, at any time, become a non-voting regular affiliate by selecting your regular affiliation class from the Bylaws and then clicking the "Star" button on this repository or alternatively downloading the ZIP file containing all of the contents of this repository.

Termination:  You may, at any time, cease being a non-voting regular affiliate by clicking the "Starred" button or renaming or deleting the ZIP file containing the contents of this repository to indicate your desire to cease being a non-voting regular affiliate.

### How do I join the Church of Open Source Software and Hardware?

It is easy!  [Read the Core Beliefs](CoreBeliefs.md) and [Read the Bylaws](BYLAWS.md).  If you agree with the Core Beliefs and the Bylaws of this Denomination of Church of Open Source Software and Hardware, then Star this repository.  If you don't have nor want to create an account on this website, you can simply download this repository instead by clicking the "Code" button above and then "Download ZIP."

### Can I join multiple Denominations of the Church of Open Source Software and Hardware at the same time?

It depends.  A Denomination may have conflicting beliefs with another Denomination, which may, in turn, have unintended legal side effects and/or consequences.  Consult with an attorney at law in your area to determine what your options are.  To avoid legal conflicts between joining multiple Denominations, it could/might be in your best interest to become a Supreme Cleric and establish your own Denomination of the Church of Open Source Software and Hardware.

### I am an open source software/hardware developer and want to support Church of Open Source Software and Hardware members and affiliates.  What can I do?

Externally to your Open Source Initiative (OSI) approved license, you can add the following language to some documentation somewhere:

> You are strongly encouraged to consult a lawyer before installing and using this software to understand any relevant laws in your area that may affect you.  Depending on the laws that may affect you from your use of this software, you may be able to gain additional Blessings, Rights, and/or legal protections by officially joining a compatible Denomination of the Church of Open Source Software and Hardware, an established religious organization that always respects and promotes your software and hardware freedoms and related civil rights and liberties.

There are many Denominations to choose from.  You can opt to recommend a few select ones that you personally like but you should leave it up to the user of your software to make their own choice.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Do not alter/modify the OSI approved license itself.

The OSI approved license you use for your software/hardware was drafted and approved by actual attorneys at law after an extensive review process.  Altering/modifying such licenses beyond the few parts you are allowed to alter/modify, usually to specify the copyright holder, may damage the license.

### What is the `Denomination #...` all about?

That is most likely the UNIX timestamp when the Denomination was first established, which helps to ensure some measure of uniqueness of the name when establishing a new Denomination.  Some Denominations, to enjoy legal protections, have to be registered with the locality/state they are established in and those localities/states require the name of a business entity to be unique across their locality/state.  Using a UNIX timestamp just happens to gives it a really large, impressive number too.

### Why?

Short answer:  Because.

Longer answer:  Some localities, states and countries are now passing laws to restrict your freedom of software and freedom of software choice in the world.  In many nations in the world, churches of established religions enjoy additional freedoms and liberties not granted to individuals nor common business entities.  Open source advocates have long promoted the ideology that open source software is the free sharing of ideas and logic in the form and expression and exercise of source code.  Open source hardware shares a similar ideology but is relatively/comparatively new in the field of open source.  The Church of Open Source Software and Hardware is merely formalizing the aforementioned ideology of open source software and hardware under the generous umbrella of religion.  This shouldn't be necessary.  However, for some insane/bonkers/wacko reason known as "some people don't understand how technology works nor understand how it has benefited them in ways they don't realize but have decided to pass laws proving those two facts," it has now become critically necessary.

### I like most of what I see but I do not fully agree with something in the Core Beliefs or Bylaws or some other reason.  What can I do?

You may opt to initiate a church split via the time-honored ceremony known as either "creating a fork" or "cloning this repository."  Creating a fork or cloning this repository begins the process of establishing a new Denomination of the Church of Open Source Software and Hardware.  To fully establish the new Denomination, you will first have to make a number of changes to this README file and the BYLAWS to officially take on the role of Supreme Cleric of your Denomination before making other desired changes.

Once you adopt the role of Supreme Cleric for your Denomination, you may also be required as the Administrative Steward (see Bylaws) by your local/state laws to register your new Denomination with specific local/state legal entities to be properly recognized as a church plant with an established religion and gain associated legal protective status as such.  You may need to consult with an attorney at law in your area to ensure full compliance with your local/state laws for your Denomination.

To help ensure the uniqueness of the name of your Denomination, you should consider denoting it with the UNIX timestamp of its creation to avoid confusion with other Denominations in your area and to expedite any legal processes (e.g. paperwork, approvals).

Finally, consider dressing up and playing organ music in the background when you push your first commit as Supreme Cleric of your Denomination.  It is not a requirement but it can be a lot of serious fun.
